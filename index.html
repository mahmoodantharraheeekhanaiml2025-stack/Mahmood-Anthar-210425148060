Interpolation Search

Binary search always checks the midpoint. Interpolation search guesses where the target should be — like flipping straight to "M" in a dictionary instead of opening in the middle. On uniformly distributed data that guess is usually right, cutting comparisons from O(log n) toward O(log log n).

pos = low + ((target − arr[low]) × (high − low)) / (arr[high] − arr[low])

the probing formula — recomputed on every step
